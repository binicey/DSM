DSM: Building A Diverse Semantic Map for 3D Visual Grounding

Qinghongbing Xie1, Zijian Liang1, Long Zeng1,,
1Tsinghua University, 2South China University of Technology
Paper arXiv Video Code Data

Video

Abstract

In recent years, with the growing research and application of multimodal large-scale language models (VLMs) in robotics. Existing approaches that use VLMs for 3D Visual Grounding tasks often focus on obtaining scene information through geometric transformations, overlooking the extrac- tion of diverse semantic information from the scene and the understanding of rich implicit semantic attributes, such as appearance, physics and affordance. The 3D scene graph, which combines geometry and language, is an ideal representation method for environmental perception and serves as an effective carrier for language models in 3D Visual Grounding tasks. To address this, we propose a multi-attribute semantic map construction method designed for robotic agents in 3D Visual Grounding tasks. This method uses VLMs to capture the latent semantic and relational attributes of objects within the scene, and creates a Diverse Semantic Map (DSM) through a temporal sliding window map construction strategy. We optimizes the understanding of Grounding information based on DSM and introduces a new approach called DSM-Grounding. Experimen- tal results demonstrate that our method outperforms existing approaches in tasks such as semantic segmentation and 3D Visual Grounding. We also deployed the algorithm on a local robot to validate its effectiveness.

Pipeline

After receiving the user's query, the robot first collects time-continuous poses, depth images, and color images of the scene to build a DSM. Next, we extract the visual and geometric information from each observation point. At the same time, we use VLM to analyze their relationships and semantic attributes, which are categorized into Appearance, Physical and Affordance Attributes. We fuse objects from multi views using a multimodal object fusion method in conjunction with the Geometry Sliding Window method for mapping. Finally, we identify candidates in the DSM based on the attributes and relationships of objects. We use the multi-level observations method to precisely locate the target object. Additionally, our method can be broadly applied to tasks such as robotic semantic navigation and semantic grasping
